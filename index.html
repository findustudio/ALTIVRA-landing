— Philosophy

Every app I tried either did one thing well, or six things badly. So I built the one I'd actually fly with.

Altivra began the way most pilot tools should begin — on a layover, in a hotel room, after a ten-hour duty. I was reconciling a duty roster from one app, a logbook from another, a fatigue tracker built in a spreadsheet, and a pay calculator I'd cobbled together over the years. Every tool knew its own job. None of them knew about each other.

The modern flight deck is a masterpiece of integration. The instruments talk. The automation understands intent. Yet the pilot's own life — our flights, our rest, our pay — lives in a patchwork of apps that haven't spoken since the sim session that introduced them.

Altivra is the refusal of that patchwork. One app. Six tools. Every module aware of every other. Your roster feeds your rest tracker. Your logbook feeds your pay verification. Your fatigue feeds your briefing. The flight deck stays uncluttered, because the deck you live with should be as elegant as the one you fly.

Frequently asked

Questions pilots ask first.

When does Altivra launch?
The private beta opens Summer 2026, prioritized for commercial pilots currently line-flying. Public launch follows once the Founding 500 have worn-tested every module at altitude and in the crew room. Join the waitlist to be first in line.
What's the difference between Command and Captain?

Command ($89/yr) is the core tier for a working pilot: unlimited logbook, auto roster import, FRMS fatigue engine, Briefing Room, pay verification, iPhone companion, priority support. If you want a single app that replaces your paper logbook, spreadsheet-based fatigue tracker, and DIY pay calculator — Command is it.

Captain ($129/yr) is everything in Command plus the two costliest-but-highest-value features: FlightAware auto-enrichment (real routing, actual block times, gate/terminal info on every flight) and the AI Copilot in Wingman — a Claude-powered assistant that understands your roster, logbook, and rest data together. Captain is priced to reflect the real per-flight and per-query costs of those premium services.

How does the Founding 500 lifetime lock-in work?

The first 500 pilots who subscribe during the private beta lock in $49/year for the Command tier — for as long as they stay subscribed. Even when public Command pricing rises after launch (and higher as the app matures), your $49 Command rate never moves.

To be fully transparent: your lock-in is specifically for the Command tier. Every new feature that ships inside Command — new reports, platform improvements, tier-internal enhancements — arrives at your Founding rate at no extra cost.

The Captain tier is a separate, higher subscription ($129/yr) that includes FlightAware enrichment and the full AI Copilot. If you ever want Captain, you can upgrade at any time by paying the tier difference, and your Command rate stays locked. Founding 500 members also get early access to Captain features as they ship.

Is my data private?
Your roster, logbook, fatigue, and pay data are yours. Stored encrypted in your iCloud so they sync across your devices and nobody else's. Never sold. Never shared with your airline. Export everything and delete your account at any time.
Does it work offline?
Yes — Altivra is offline-first. Every module works at FL380, in the crew room, at a layover hotel on bad Wi-Fi. Changes sync automatically when you're back online. You never lose a logbook entry to spotty connectivity.
What iPad do I need?
Any iPad running iPadOS 17 or newer. Altivra is built natively with SwiftUI — it feels fast on an iPad Mini and looks gorgeous on a 13" Pro. If your airline already approved your iPad as an EFB, you're good.
Will it import my airline's roster?
Yes — that's the whole point of Wingman. We support ICS calendar feeds (works with almost every crew system), PDF roster parsing, and native integrations with popular crew-management platforms. If yours isn't supported yet, tell us — adding one is usually a few days of work.
Is it EFB-approved?
Altivra is not a certified EFB — it's a personal productivity app for pilots. It lives alongside your company EFB (Jeppesen, Lido, ForeFlight) and handles the parts those apps don't touch: your personal logbook, rest, pay, and career data. Some airlines allow Altivra on the same iPad as your EFB; check with your operator.
Can I import my existing logbook?
Yes. CSV import from LogTen Pro, ForeFlight Logbook, MyFlightBook, and any spreadsheet format. The importer handles common aircraft type codes and airport codes automatically, and flags anything ambiguous for you to confirm.
Who is Altivra built for?
Commercial pilots line-flying jets. Regional and major airlines. Long-haul and short-haul. If you spend your career bridging roster systems, paper logbooks, fatigue spreadsheets, and pay calculators — Altivra was built specifically for you.
Can I pay monthly?
Yes — Command is $89/year or $8.99/month. Captain is annual only ($129/yr) because the FlightAware and AI Copilot cost structure favors annual commitments. The Founding 500 tier is annual only too — it's a commitment to the early journey in exchange for lifetime pricing.
What are the limits on the Free tier?
Free lets you try the deck, not live on it. You can log up to 50 flights, view your roster in read-only mode, see basic career stats, browse the world airport map, and ask 3 AI Copilot queries per month. PDF exports are watermarked. There's no trial clock — take as long as you want. When you're ready for auto-roster, FRMS fatigue, and pay verification, Command is one tap away. When you want FlightAware enrichment and the full AI Copilot, Captain is the upgrade path.
What is the AI Copilot in Wingman?
An AI assistant, powered by Claude, that understands your roster, logbook, and rest data together. Ask questions in plain English — "when's my next long-haul?", "how many layovers in Tokyo this quarter?", "do I have enough rest for the LHR pairing?" — and get instant answers. It's a Captain-tier feature arriving in 2026. Free and Command get 3 sample queries per month; Captain gets unlimited. Founding 500 pilots get early access.
How does FlightAware enrichment work?
Every flight you log is automatically matched against FlightAware's real-time flight data — actual routing, gate and terminal assignments, real block times, diversions. You don't have to enter anything manually beyond the flight number and date. The enrichment runs server-side so your iPad isn't making API calls. It's a Captain-tier feature (the per-flight API cost is why it lives in the premium tier).
Will prices go up after launch?
Yes — deliberately. The $89/yr Command rate is intentionally priced under a single sim-session cost during the beta. Once the app matures past public launch, Command will rise to $99/yr (and potentially higher over time). Captain starts at $129/yr and is unlikely to drop. Founding 500 members are immune to all future increases — the $49/yr rate is locked in on the Command tier for as long as you stay subscribed.
